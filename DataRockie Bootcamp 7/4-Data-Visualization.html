Chart 1

ggplot(diamonds %>% sample_n(1000), aes(carat, price, col=cut)) +
         geom_point(size=2, alpha=0.7) +
         theme_light() +
         labs(
           title = "Relationship between carat and price by cut",
           x = "Carat",
           y = "Price USD",
           caption = "Datasource: diamonds ggplot2"
         ) +
         scale_color_brewer(type="qual", palette=1)

We found a positive relationship.

Chart 2

ggplot(diamonds %>% sample_n(1000), aes(price)) +
  geom_histogram(bins = 10, color="black", fill="skyblue") +
  theme_light() +
  labs(
    title = "Price distribution distinguish by cut quality",
    caption = "Datasource: diamonds ggplot2"
  ) +
  facet_wrap(~cut, ncol=5)

We found positive skewed distribution every cut quality.

Mostly diamonds have low price after all.
